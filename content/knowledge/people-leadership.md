# People leadership for technical executives

## Hiring and bar raising

Describe how you define levels, calibrate interview loops, and reduce bias without slowing decisions. Strong answers include how you coach interviewers and how you repair mistakes when a hire does not work out.

## Performance and dignity

- Expectations should be observable: what great looks like in outcomes and behaviors.
- Hard feedback early prevents slow drift and resentment.
- Performance improvement plans are a last resort, not a surprise attack.

## Operating cadence

Share your rhythm: one-on-ones, staff meetings, cross-functional forums, and how information flows upward without becoming theater.

## Distributed and hybrid realities

Be explicit about documentation norms, timezone fairness, and how you keep remote contributors visible for growth opportunities.
