## SECTION 7 — EXECUTIVE BEHAVIOURAL & PRESSURE QUESTIONS

### MASTER QUESTION — “Tell us about yourself” (executive-level)

This is the most important answer in your entire interview.

Because before they evaluate your strategy, cybersecurity knowledge, leadership, operations discipline, or financial maturity, they first decide:

**Who are you professionally?**

This answer shapes everything.

A weak answer creates a weak interview. A strong answer controls the interview from the beginning.

Most candidates fail because they give **life history**, **CV reading**, or a **random career timeline.** Wrong.

This answer must establish **executive identity** — not biography.

## The executive framework

Use:

**WHO YOU ARE → WHAT YOU SOLVE → WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT → WHY THIS ROLE**

This is the strongest structure.

## Perfect master answer (executive version)

“I am an enterprise IT leader focused on building resilient infrastructure, operational stability, and technology environments that allow businesses to scale without failure.

Over the past 10+ years, I have led multi-site IT operations across Africa, supporting over 2,000 users across more than 15 locations while maintaining high service availability and strong operational discipline.

My experience covers infrastructure strategy, network transformation, cybersecurity governance, vendor management, business continuity, and executive-level IT leadership.

A large part of my work has been around solving complex operational problems—reducing downtime, improving customer experience, strengthening resilience, and ensuring IT decisions directly support business growth rather than simply maintaining systems.

For example, I led centralized network transformation across multiple sites, reducing network-related incidents by over 80%, improving visibility, strengthening security controls, and delivering significant annual cost savings through vendor consolidation and smarter infrastructure design.

I also implemented operational improvements like cloud printing systems that improved customer experience while generating over 6 million KES monthly in new revenue, which reflects how I approach IT—not just as support, but as a business enabler.

What makes me different is that I combine deep technical understanding with strong business thinking. I am comfortable discussing infrastructure and cybersecurity, but I lead through business outcomes—risk reduction, customer trust, operational continuity, and financial discipline.

I work best in environments where IT is expected to be strategic, accountable, and closely aligned to business performance.

That is why senior leadership roles are the right fit for me—because they allow me to create long-term value at scale, not just solve isolated technical problems.”

## Shorter version (concise panels)

“I am an enterprise IT leader focused on building resilient infrastructure and operational environments that support business growth without failure.

I have over 10 years of experience leading multi-site IT operations across Africa, supporting 2,000+ users across 15+ locations with strong focus on uptime, cybersecurity, business continuity, vendor governance, and strategic transformation.

My strength is aligning technology with business outcomes—reducing downtime, improving customer experience, strengthening resilience, and delivering measurable financial value.

For example, I led centralized network transformation that reduced incidents by over 80% and delivered major cost savings, and I also introduced service improvements like cloud printing systems that generated new revenue while improving customer experience.

I believe strong IT leadership is not about managing infrastructure alone—it is about protecting operations, reducing risk, and enabling business growth through technology.”

## Executive rules for this answer

**Rule 1 — Never start with education**

**Wrong:** “I studied…” (too junior.)

**Start with:** professional identity.

**Rule 2 — Never read your CV**

They already have it. Your job is **positioning** — not repetition.

**Rule 3 — Must include measurable wins**

Executives trust numbers. Use: **80% reduction**, **2,000+ users**, **15+ locations**, **6M monthly revenue**, **millions saved** — where true to your story.

**Rule 4 — Must sound strategic**

**Weak:** “I manage IT support.”

**Strong:** “I align IT to business continuity and growth.” Huge difference.

**Rule 5 — End with senior positioning**

The close must make the panel think: **yes, this person belongs at this level.** That ending matters.
