# Infrastructure and platform strategy

## Reliability is a product

Executives expect you to treat reliability like a product with owners, budgets, and customer-visible commitments. SLOs are not academic exercises; they are negotiation tools between engineering and the business.

## Modernization without magical thinking

- Inventory what is expensive to change versus expensive to operate.
- Sequence migrations so each step reduces risk or cost, not just rearranges it.
- Standardize golden paths for the majority; carve exceptions deliberately and visibly.

## FinOps that engineers respect

Show how you connect unit economics to architecture: autoscaling policies, storage tiers, reserved capacity where it is justified, and continuous rightsizing as a habit.

## Story beats that land

- A measurable reduction in incident frequency or severity after a structural change.
- A migration completed without a big-bang outage because you engineered rollback and rehearsal.
