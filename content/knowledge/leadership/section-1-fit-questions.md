- Head of IT

- Senior IT Manager

## 2. What do you know about our company and business model?

### Perfect Answer

Before any interview, I believe understanding the business is as important as understanding the technology.

Your organization operates in a highly service-driven environment where operational continuity, customer experience, and speed of execution are critical. Technology is not simply a support function — it directly affects revenue, customer trust, compliance, and long-term competitiveness.

What stands out to me is the scale of operations, the importance of reliability, and the need for IT to function as both an operational backbone and a strategic enabler. Whether it is logistics, financial services, enterprise operations, or development-focused institutions, the common denominator is that downtime has direct business consequences.

I also understand that modern organizations are prioritizing cybersecurity maturity, automation, cost optimization, and data-driven decision-making. This means the Head of IT role must go beyond infrastructure management and focus on resilience, governance, and innovation.

That is where I believe my experience aligns strongly — building environments where uptime, security, and business value are all treated as equally important.

## 5. What do you understand about this role?

### Perfect Answer

I understand this role as much more than managing infrastructure or resolving technical issues.

At senior level, the Head of IT is responsible for ensuring that technology supports the organization’s operational continuity, strategic growth, and risk management.

This includes maintaining reliable infrastructure, strong cybersecurity posture, vendor governance, compliance, disaster recovery readiness, and high-quality IT service delivery.

It also means building strong partnerships with Finance, Operations, HR, and executive leadership so that IT decisions are aligned with broader business objectives.

The role requires balancing innovation with operational stability — ensuring the business remains secure and reliable today while also preparing for tomorrow through automation, modernization, and better data visibility.

Ultimately, the Head of IT is accountable for making technology a source of confidence for the business, not a source of uncertainty.

## 6. Where do you see the IT function in the next 3 years?

### Perfect Answer

In the next three years, I see the IT function moving even further from support operations into strategic business leadership.

Traditional IT focused heavily on infrastructure uptime and user support. That remains important, but modern IT must also drive automation, predictive decision-making, cybersecurity resilience, and business innovation.

I see three major priorities:

- **Stronger operational resilience** — through proactive monitoring, automation, and improved disaster recovery readiness.
- **Deeper cybersecurity maturity** — where security becomes embedded into operations rather than treated as a separate function. Identity management, compliance, and cyber resilience will be major leadership priorities.
- **Intelligent automation and AI adoption** — using technology to reduce repetitive work, improve service management, and enhance customer and employee experience.

I also believe IT leaders will increasingly be judged by business outcomes such as productivity, cost optimization, customer satisfaction, and risk reduction — not just technical uptime.

The future of IT is not support. It is strategic enablement.
