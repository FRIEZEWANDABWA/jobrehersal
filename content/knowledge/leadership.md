# Executive IT leadership

## What interviewers are really testing

They are not checking whether you memorized frameworks. They want evidence that you can translate technology into outcomes, align diverse stakeholders, and remain steady when information is incomplete.

## Narrative spine for senior roles

- Start with the business outcome, then reveal the technical path.
- Name trade-offs explicitly: speed versus risk, cost versus resilience, build versus buy.
- Show how you created clarity: decision records, operating rhythms, dashboards that executives actually use.

## Questions you should be able to answer cold

- When did you disagree with a peer executive, and how did you carry the decision forward?
- How do you prioritize when every initiative claims to be critical?
- Describe a transformation where you inherited a struggling team.

## Pressure interview posture

Short answers feel evasive. Long monologues feel unstructured. Aim for ninety seconds with a clear arc: context, your move, measurable result, what you would refine next time.
