# Finance fluency for technology leaders

## Speak the language of portfolios

Be comfortable with operating versus capital expenditure, depreciation assumptions, and how multi-year contracts interact with cash planning. You do not need to be an accountant; you need to be credible in the room where budgets are decided.

## Business cases that survive scrutiny

- Anchor on outcomes and risks avoided, not only on feature lists.
- Show sensitivity analysis: what happens if adoption is slower or costs drift upward?
- Tie funding milestones to measurable decision points.

## Procurement and vendor leverage

Describe how you run competitive processes, manage renewal cycles, and create alternatives so negotiations are grounded in reality rather than brinkmanship.

## Red flags interviewers listen for

Promising fixed savings without assumptions, ignoring switching costs, or treating headcount reduction as the only success metric.
