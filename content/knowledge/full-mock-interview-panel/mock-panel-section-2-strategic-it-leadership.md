# SECTION 2 — STRATEGIC IT LEADERSHIP

Questions **6–8**

---

### 6. How do you align IT strategy with business objectives?

**Elite answer**

“I align IT strategy by starting with business priorities, not technology priorities.

The first question is always: what is limiting growth, customer trust, operational efficiency, or resilience?

From there, I translate business needs into technology priorities—whether infrastructure modernization, cybersecurity investment, service delivery improvement, or operational visibility.

I use clear roadmaps with measurable outcomes so leadership can see how IT investment improves business performance rather than existing as separate technical activity.

IT strategy should never be a parallel plan. It should be the execution engine behind business strategy.”

---

### 7. How do you balance innovation with stability?

**Elite answer**

“I believe stability comes before innovation because unstable environments cannot support meaningful transformation.

My approach is to first secure operational reliability—availability, security posture, vendor accountability, and recovery confidence.

Once the foundation is stable, innovation becomes safer and faster because the business can absorb change without disruption.

I evaluate innovation through business value, risk exposure, adoption readiness, and governance—not technology excitement.

Innovation should improve resilience and growth, not create new operational fragility.

The goal is controlled modernization, not change for appearance.”

---

### 8. What would your first 90 days look like?

**Elite answer**

“My first 90 days would focus on listening, visibility, and controlled prioritization.

First, I would assess service health, critical risks, stakeholder confidence, vendor performance, cybersecurity posture, and team capability.

Second, I would stabilize any major operational pain points affecting customer trust, business continuity, or executive confidence.

Third, I would identify quick wins that improve visibility and trust—whether around incident discipline, vendor accountability, or reporting clarity.

From there, I would define a practical roadmap aligned to business priorities, balancing immediate resilience with long-term transformation.

The goal of the first 90 days is not dramatic change—it is trusted control.”
