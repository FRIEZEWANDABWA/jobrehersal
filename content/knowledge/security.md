# Security at executive altitude

## The shift from operator to owner

At executive levels, you are judged on how you fund security work, how you govern third parties, and how you communicate incidents without creating panic or hiding material facts.

## Defensible storylines

- Identity as the control plane: least privilege, break-glass, lifecycle for machine identities.
- Data protection that respects usability: classification, handling rules, monitoring that reduces false positives over time.
- Supply chain realism: SBOM where it matters, vendor attestations, and pragmatic acceptance criteria for procurement.

## Incident leadership without theatrics

- Stabilize, communicate, learn: a steady tempo beats heroic improvisation.
- Separate technical facts from customer and regulatory obligations early.
- Capture lessons as durable controls, not as one-off heroics.

## What to avoid

Claiming zero breaches, blaming users, or treating compliance frameworks as substitutes for engineering judgment.
